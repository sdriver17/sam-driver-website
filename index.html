SAM DRIVER

BIO

Sam is a senior Theater and Computer science major who is passionate about harnessing his creativity in all aspects of his work.

As an artist he is interested in finding the intersection between theater and technology, trying to create and be a part of new work using modern techniques to create inventive final products. In addition to his broad artistic training at Wesleyan University, Sam received classical acting training from some of the most renowned teachers in England during his time at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts. Sam has extensive experience on the technical side of theater, although he specializes in lighting design. This winter, Sam was the lighting designer for Above The Noise, a new work at Dixon Place in New York City. His performance in Wesleyan University's Prodcution of The Masses Are Asses will release this may.

Sam is proficient in many different coding languages, including SML, Java/JavaScript, and C/C#. He has worked as a Backend Engineer for several companies, and has always enjoyed building a product as a part of a team. Recently, Sam and a colleague have completed research into Two-Factor Authentication and Phishing Attacks. This research can be viewed here.
